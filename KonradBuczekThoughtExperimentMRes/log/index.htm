Konrad Buczek Thought Experiment‌‌


1.


My first reaction to the text “Going to the Hairdresser” and the process itself was met with fear and how much I hate getting my hair cut. Yet it is a social ritual that most of us engage in.


2.


I was critical of how the author of my assignment omits emotions in a broader spectrum of the. The topic of emotions became more apparent after my tutorial session where we all agreed on how changing your hair can affect you as much if not more on the inside as the outside.


3.


Finding my Passion by “Going to the Hairdresser.”


Extending the notion of emotions to how hair is part of you and your identity.


Notes:

  • The same process of cutting, extending, colouring (experimenting), replacing etc.

  • Finding (forming) a big part of your identity

  • Accepting change (baldness, grey hair) and what looks best/hair type (curls, natural colour)

  • How it relates to everything else internal/external (face shape, environment)


4.


After discussing at my tutorials the huge role sound plays at the hair salon and how terrifying razor sound is to the human ear I started reading up on the opposite and the way deaf people experience having their hair cut. It can be terrifying when you are not able to hear.

5.


Research on sound and solutions for the deaf proofed to be difficult as establishing an accurate conception of sound is somewhat harder than I thought. As observed the chief reason for this difficulty originates from the complex relationship between sound as a physical form and sound as a sensual experience.

I still thought about having the audience wear ear plugs but dismissed my first idea for the presentation due to some risks.‌

6.


When generating new ideas I deciding that I still wanted to use sound in a way the audience would experience it while showing how horrifying getting your hair cut can be when you don't hear anything.

7.


Technology research phase on how I can get my idea across. After conducting research it became clear that the key tools I would use are surrounded around technological advancements in Virtual and Augmented Reality.


Stereo can now use dual sensors to extract 3D information from a scene.


Using depth technology in a similar way to how human vision works, stereo depth is calculated using two sensors and our efficient on-board vision processor for low power and high performance.


For completing the presentation effect I would use cameras capable of providing Virtual Reality filming and appropriate sound if necessary.
